Campaign Criteria and Terms of Service
Your access to and use of OurDemocracy is conditional upon your acceptance and compliance with the following terms:
  • Tax liabilities will be the sole responsibility of the applicant.
  • All contributions are voluntary in nature and non-refundable.
  • All funds will be transferred directly into the personal account of the beneficiary only. Funds will not be transferred into the account of any third party.
  • Campaigner must take responsibility for all the coordination with the beneficiary or whoever they are raising funds for.
  • If the campaigner and beneficiary are different entities, the campaigner must provide written consent from the beneficiary. The applicantâ€™s use of the OurDemocracy platform must be only for legal and authorized purposes.
  • The applicant shall not use the OurDemocracy platform in any way which is illegal, obscene, defamatory, threatening, harassing, racially or ethnically hateful or offensive, or instigates conduct that is illegal or amounts to promotion of violent crimes.
  • The applicant shall not undertake any campaign, the objective of which is inconsistent with the spirit and provisions of the Constitution of India or is in violation of existing law.
  • Campaign approval is the sole discretion of Our Democracy platform admin.
General criteria to start a campaign:
  • The applicant should be 18 years or above
  • The applicant should be an Indian citizen
  • Beneficiary should be an Indian citizen
Criteria for Media campaigns (In addition to the general criteria mentioned above):
  • Journalist or media organisation should be based in India.
  • Campaigns are not just limited to Journalists or media organisations. Anyone with a genuine story idea or a project can apply, subject to approval by Our Democracy admin.
Criteria for Electoral campaigns
  • Applicant raising funds must provide additional documents, recommendations and written undertaking to Our Democracy admin before the campaign is approved.
  • Applicant must be a candidate contesting elections in India to the Parliament, State Legislative Assembly or urban/rural local bodies.
  • Applicant contesting elections must fulfill all qualifications laid down by law, to be eligible to contest elections to the Parliament or State Legislative Assembly.
  • Applicant contesting elections must not be disqualified by law, from contesting elections to Parliament, state or urban/rural local bodies.
  • Applicants must undertake to conduct their campaign in accordance with election law, abide by the letter and spirit of the Model Code of Conduct and maintain a true and correct account of their election expenses in the manner prescribed by law.
